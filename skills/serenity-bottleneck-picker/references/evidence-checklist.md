# Evidence Checklist

Use this file when a candidate sounds plausible but evidence quality is unclear.

## Minimum Standard

Before a candidate becomes investable research, require at least two independent evidence types, with at least one confirmed or strong source:

- Product evidence: datasheet, teardown, named product line, technical presentation, validated spec.
- Customer evidence: qualification, design win, supplier list, shipment, purchase order, customer-side confirmation.
- Financial evidence: revenue ramp, backlog, margin mix shift, ASP increase, utilization, capex tied to the bottleneck.
- Industry evidence: standards change, procurement bottleneck, lead-time extension, credible dated channel work.
- Capacity evidence: new line, subsidy, equipment order, hiring, factory utilization, expansion schedule.

## Evidence Stage

```text
0 = pure concept
1 = R&D / demo
2 = qualification
3 = small-batch shipment
4 = production ramp
5 = revenue already visible
```

Best early windows usually sit at stage 2-4. Stage 0-1 is lottery only. Stage 5 may still work, but market recognition risk is higher.

## Red Flags

- Company says "AI ecosystem" but does not name the product, process, customer, or qualification path.
- Exposure is less than 5-10% of revenue and no path to materiality exists.
- Revenue growth is unrelated to the claimed bottleneck.
- The company is a distributor/reseller but is valued like the owner of the scarce node.
- The thesis depends on one investor slide or one social-media post.
- Serial ATM, convertibles, or emergency financing fund the story.
- Stock already re-rated 5x-10x before financial validation.

## A-Share Extra Checks

- Separate "has product" from "has qualified customer."
- Separate "sample sent" from "volume shipment."
- Check subsidies versus actual customer demand.
- Check related-party orders and vague investor Q&A language.
- Check turnover percentile, 龙虎榜 institutional/hot-money split, company abnormal-move warnings, and late-cycle laggard rotation.
